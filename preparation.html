Preparation & Training

The path to becoming a professional plumber

Education & Training

No college degree is required but proper training and certification are essential for becoming a plumber.

  • High School Diploma or GED: Basic requirement for entering the field.
  • Trade School or Vocational Program: Provides basic foundational knowledge and hands-on training.
  • Apprenticeship Programs: Typically 4-5 years, combining paid on-the-job training with basic skill learning.

Required Skills

Plumbers need a mix of technical and soft skills to succeed in their careers.

  • Strong problem-solving and troubleshooting skills.
  • Knowledge of plumbing systems, tools, and safety protocols.
  • Physical stamina and manual dexterity.
  • Customer service and communication skills for interacting with clients.

Certifications & Licensing

Plumbers must meet licensing and certification requirements based on their location.

  • Journeyman Plumber License: Typically requires apprenticeship completion and an exam.
  • Master Plumber Certification: For experienced plumbers looking to start their own business.
  • Specialized Certifications: Such as Green Plumbing or Medical Gas Certification.

Getting Started

Here are actionable steps to begin a plumbing career:

  • Complete a vocational training program or apprenticeship.
  • Gain hands-on experience under licensed plumbers.
  • Pass necessary licensing exams in your state.
  • Continue education to specialize and stay updated on new technologies.
